# Roadmap

Distilled from a full-project critique (2026-07-02): architecture, robustness, and user-workflow reviews. Work proceeds **one sprint at a time** ‚Äî see the working agreement in `CLAUDE.md`. Each sprint is sized to roughly one working session. Don't renumber sprints; mark items done (`~~strikethrough~~` or ‚úÖ) and log outcomes in `ISSUES.md`.

**The through-line:** the form is excellent at *collecting* (landing, wizard, photo questionnaire) but treats *assembling and delivering the packet* as the user's problem ‚Äî and the HOA's own rules make incompleteness unforgiving (auto-denial, 45-day clock never starts). Sprints 1‚Äì2 fix that. Sprints 3‚Äì5 pay down robustness, code-health, and accessibility debt.

**Diagnosis (reasoned through in the 2026-07-05 conversation, not yet verified in code):** `buildAerialNode()` (`plot-editor.js`, ~L881) registers the fetched aerial photo to the drawn parcel outline via `fitSimilarity()` (`geometry.js` L59) ‚Äî a least-squares rigid fit (rotation + uniform scale + translation) between two point sets that come from **two different map projections**: the grid's own parcel outline (`parcelPolygonPx`, from `buildParcelGrid()` in `geometry.js`, which projects via a local flat-earth tangent plane / `geoToLocalMeters()` so "1 cell = 1 real foot" holds exactly) vs. the aerial image's parcel-vertex pixels (`plotBgParcelPx`, from `fetchAerialBackdrop()` in `map-wizard.js`, which projects via true Web Mercator / `lngLatToMercator()`, since that's what the county ImageServer's `bboxSR=3857` export expects). Any two distinct projections agree only approximately over a small area, and ‚Äî this is the key mechanism ‚Äî a least-squares similarity fit is anchored at the point-set centroid, so whatever small residual exists between the two projections is **linearly amplified with distance from that centroid**. A tiny (sub-percent) residual is invisible on a small, compact, roughly-square lot; the same residual is very visible at the far vertices of a bigger or more irregularly-shaped lot (e.g. a pie-shaped cul-de-sac parcel).
