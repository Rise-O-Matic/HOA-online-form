# Submission-process email (Carolmarie Taylor / FSResidential)

**What this is.** The email FSResidential sent to owners alongside the updated
application form (`docs/source-form.pdf`, "Updated May 2026") to explain the new
submission process. The user supplied it verbatim on 2026-07-13 and asked that it be
kept next to the source PDF — it resolves several open questions in
`docs/policy-docket.md` that the form alone left ambiguous. Exact send date not given;
treat it as contemporaneous with the May 2026 form update unless corrected.

Quoted verbatim, no edits:

---

> Good morning,
>
> To eliminate any confusion regarding the updated architectural application submission
> process, please note the following requirements:
>
> **1. New Application Format Required**
> All architectural applications must be submitted using the new, updated form. Old
> versions will not be accepted under any circumstances.
>
> **2. Submission Contact**
> All applications must be submitted only to Carol Taylor, Sr. ARCH Specialist. No other
> staff member will be accepting applications.
>
> **3. Incomplete Applications**
> Any application that is incomplete will be returned. It is essential that you review
> all required information before submitting.
> If an application is submitted on the deadline date and is incomplete, it will not be
> reviewed. You will be required to resubmit the following month. No exceptions will be
> made.
> If you are unsure whether your application is complete, we strongly recommend calling
> the office to schedule an appointment with the ARCH Specialist before the deadline.
> Carol will not be available for meetings on the deadline day.
>
> **4. Payment Process**
> Please do not call or come into the office to make payment. Payment will only be taken
> after the ARCH Specialist has reviewed your application and confirmed it is complete.
> Carol will contact you directly to collect the application fee, or you may provide card
> details in your submission email. A receipt will be emailed to the email address on
> file. Payment will not be processed for incomplete applications.
> If your application is returned, you must call the office to schedule an appointment to
> review what information is missing.
>
> The updated application is attached. To schedule an appointment with Carol, please call
> 951-801-4246.

---

## What this settles in `docs/policy-docket.md`

- **Item 6a** ("older or printed versions not accepted") — **confirmed**, verbatim:
  "Old versions will not be accepted under any circumstances."
- **Item 6b** ("no other staff member will accept applications") — **confirmed**,
  verbatim: "No other staff member will be accepting applications."
- **Item 6c** ("Carol not available for meetings on the deadline day") — **confirmed**,
  verbatim: "Carol will not be available for meetings on the deadline day."
- **Item 2** (fee timing) — **confirms** the shipped provisional position: payment is
  requested only after completeness is confirmed, collected by phone/card-in-email, never
  at the office counter, never for an incomplete application.
- **Item 3** (incomplete-application outcome) — **adds a detail neither page 1 nor page 6
  states alone**: an incomplete application is "returned" generally (implying it can be
  fixed and resubmitted within the cycle), but if incomplete *on the deadline date itself*
  it gets no review that cycle and must wait for the next month — "no exceptions." This
  reads as a middle ground between the source's two conflicting phrasings (page 1's
  "review period doesn't start" vs. page 6's "returned and automatically denied"): dying
  isn't guaranteed by mere incompleteness, but incompleteness *at the deadline* is fatal
  for that cycle.
- **New signal for the Sprint 29 "office intake block" question** — the whole process
  described here is email-submission + phone-appointment, with payment explicitly kept
  off the office counter. There is no physical-intake moment described anywhere. This
  weighs against printing a "STAFF ONLY — date received / date paid" stamp block on the
  applicant's packet (see `ROADMAP.md` Sprint 29).
